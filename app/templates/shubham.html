Introduction

S&P 500, short for Standard and Poor's 500, is a stock market index that measures the performance of roughly the 500 largest comapnies listed on stock exchanges in the United States. It is seen as a good measure of the health of the US stock market and US economy as a whole. Hence, each stock pick in the S&P 500 serves an elevated role in possibly influencing overall investor and consumer sentiment especially near times of crisis. Let's take a look a deeper look inside the S&P 500 index to see what insights we can draw about the US economy prior to the coronavirus pandemic.

S&P 500 Composition

In the dashboard above, we can see the overall breakdown of the S&P 500 into various sectors. In the top visualization we can see that if we were to purely just look at the number of occurences of each sector, the Finance sector accounts for nearly 1/5 of the entire index with a total of 95 companies. Hence it might seem that Finance companies play the biggest role in shaping the US Stock Market.

However, if we are to take market cap into consideration as we do in the bottom visualization, the Computers and Technology sector is overwhelmingly dominant with the Finance and Medical sector being a distant 2nd and 3rd place. Inspecting the base of the Computer and Technology category in the bar chart, reveals that this category has companies such as Apple, Microsoft, and Google which account for around a market cap of 1 Trillion Dollars each, which can help explain the high value of the sector as a whole. This key market cap factor is important since a company's market cap helps determine its weight in the index and hence its weight in shaping the stock market. Hence if covid-19 has a large impact on these big sectors, it would take a heavy toll on the S&P 500.

We can see that the top 8 sectors in the S&P 500 account for an overwhelming majority of the index both in terms of numbers and market cap. Lets take a look at these sectors a bit closer to see what makes them such key pillars of the US stock market and economy.

Stock Revenues by Sector

Revenue refers to the total income earned by a company before taking into account any expenses. Increaings revenue over time can indicate whether a company or sector is growing and capturing more of its market. From the line chart, we can see that all the 8 major sectors of the S&P 500 have increased their revenue year over year since 2017. The Retail-Wholesale sector generates the highest revenue on average every year, rougly between 50 and 60 billion dollars annually with top performers being Walmart, Amazon, and CVS. However, looking at revenue growth, we can see that the Computer and Technology and Finance sector performed the best, growing revenue by rougly 23% and 19% respectively.

However, looking at revenue growth is not enough. Let's analyze the earnings growth of our top S&P 500 sectors to see how much money is being made available in the bottom line which could be reinvested into the business or distributed to shareholders as dividends.

Stock Earnings by Sector

Earnings refer to the net income earned by a company after taking all expenses into account. Increasing earnings over time can indicate that a company is becoming more mature and more profitable by optimizing its operation costs. From the line chart, we can see that things are not as clear cut as they were with revenue. Of the 8 sectors we are observing, the Consumer Discretionary, Utilities, and Consumer Staples sectors observe negative earnings growth in the last 3 years. We observe that the Computer and Technology sector has the largest earnings every year with Apple being the largest earner. However, looking at the percent earnings change over the last 3 years, the medical sector had the strongest performance with a near 68% increase in revenue.

Notice that overall, the biggest sectors in the stock market, Computer and Technology, Finance, and Medical, have all been increasing in revenues and earnings over the last few years. Hence, prior to the covid-19 pandemic, investors had been pricing in a very strong 2020 earnings, which pushed the overall S&P 500 stock prices higher and higher.
